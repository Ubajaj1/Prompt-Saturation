\section{Judge-Scored Marginal Contributions (Artefact Reference)}
\label{app:judge-marginal}

Figure~\ref{fig:judge-marginal} shows the per-layer marginal contributions for
the four ground-truth tasks scored by the LLM judge, the counterpart to the
deterministic Figure~\ref{fig:marginal} in the main text.
The contrast is the point: under the judge, product-extraction gain appears
spread across the field-name layer (L1$\to$L2) and the worked example
(L6$\to$L7), implying a ``distributed'' profile.
The deterministic metric (Figure~\ref{fig:marginal}) overturns this, assigning
92\% of the gain to the single L3 JSON-format layer and essentially nothing to
the worked example.
This is the evaluation artefact discussed in
Section~\ref{sec:result-judge}: the judge awards partial credit to malformed
output, which both masks the L3 step and cosmetically rewards the worked
example.

\section{Output Length Control}
\label{app:output}

Output length varies across levels. We computed partial correlations
controlling for output length to confirm the prompt$\to$quality relationship
is not an artefact of output compression.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Partial correlations controlling for output length.}
\label{tab:partial}
\small
\begin{tabular}{lrr}
\toprule
Task & $r$(output, quality) & partial $r$(prompt, quality $|$ output) \\
\midrule
Classification      & $-0.186$ & $0.106$ \\
Product extraction  & $-0.313$ & $0.447$ \\
Instr.\ following   & $0.616$  & $0.209$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The prompt$\to$quality relationship persists after controlling for output
length (positive partial $r$ for all three tasks).

\section{Replication Experiment}
\label{app:replication}

\begin{figure}[h]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/fig_replication_curves.png}
  \caption{200-example replication (llama-3.1-8b): classification shows clear
    saturation at $\approx$64 tokens; QA shows no systematic improvement with
    prompt length.}
  \label{fig:replication}
\end{figure}

The 200-example replication on SST-2 (classification) and SQuAD (QA)
confirms the main-study findings (Figure~\ref{fig:replication}).
Classification saturates at approximately 64 tokens with a narrow bootstrap
CI, while QA shows no statistically significant relationship between prompt
length and quality.
